🧠 Try Free AI Tools for Fake News Detection

Explore these powerful tools to help you analyze content and identify potential misinformation.

GLTR

Giant Language model Test Room detects if text was generated by AI by analyzing statistical patterns in the text that humans wouldn't normally produce.

Try GLTR

GPTZero

Identifies AI-generated content through advanced detection algorithms. Great for checking if essays, articles, or news were written by humans or AI.

Try GPTZero

InVID Plugin

A browser extension that helps verify the authenticity of videos and images online through reverse image search and metadata analysis.

Try InVID

TinEye

Reverse image search engine that finds where an image appears online, helping you identify the original source and any modifications.

Try TinEye

ClaimBuster

Uses AI to identify fact-checkable claims in text and helps verify their accuracy against a database of checked facts.

Try ClaimBuster

CrossCheck

Collaborative verification platform where journalists work together to debunk rumors, misinformation, and fake news.

Try CrossCheck

Test It Yourself

Upload content or paste text to analyze it for signs of AI generation or misinformation.

About TruthLens

Fighting misinformation through education and accessible tools.

Our Mission

TruthLens was created to empower everyone with the tools and knowledge to identify fake news and misinformation. In today's digital landscape, the ability to verify information is a critical skill, and we're committed to making these tools accessible to all.

"We built TruthLens because we believe that media literacy and fact-checking should be available to everyone, not just journalists and researchers. Our success with Dot Lebanon and GIL Project shows there's a real need for these tools in our communities."
